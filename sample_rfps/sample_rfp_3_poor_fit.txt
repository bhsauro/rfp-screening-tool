REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL
Highway Infrastructure Design and Construction Management Services

Issuing Organization: California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)
RFP Number: CALTRANS-CE-2026-1842
Issue Date: April 22, 2026
Submission Deadline: May 10, 2026 (4:00 PM PST)

1. PROJECT SUMMARY

The California Department of Transportation is seeking proposals from qualified civil engineering and construction management firms to provide design and construction oversight services for the Interstate 5 Corridor Widening Project in Los Angeles County.

2. PROJECT OVERVIEW

Scope: Design and construction management for 8.5 miles of highway expansion, including:

- Widening I-5 from 4 lanes to 6 lanes in each direction
- Design of 3 new freeway interchanges
- Replacement of 2 aging overpasses
- Stormwater drainage system upgrades
- Sound wall installation (4.2 miles)
- Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) infrastructure integration
- Environmental mitigation and habitat restoration
- Traffic management during construction phases

Total Project Value: $485 million (design and construction combined)

3. SCOPE OF WORK FOR SELECTED FIRM

The consulting firm will provide:

- Complete highway geometric design and engineering plans
- Structural engineering for bridges and overpasses
- Geotechnical investigation and foundation design
- Hydraulic and drainage system engineering
- Environmental compliance and permitting support
- Construction cost estimating and value engineering
- Full-time construction management and inspection services
- Quality assurance/quality control oversight
- Coordination with local jurisdictions and utilities
- Public outreach and stakeholder engagement

4. PROJECT TIMELINE

Phase 1 - Design: 18 months (June 2026 - December 2027)
Phase 2 - Construction Management: 36 months (January 2028 - December 2030)
Total Duration: 54 months

5. MANDATORY QUALIFICATIONS

Prime consultant must have:

- Licensed Professional Civil Engineer (PE) in California with highway design experience
- Minimum 15 years experience in major highway/freeway design projects
- Experience as prime consultant on minimum 3 projects >$100M construction value
- Demonstrated expertise in Caltrans design standards and procedures
- In-house structural engineering capabilities (bridge design)
- CADD capabilities using Caltrans-approved software (MicroStation, Geopak)
- Experience with NEPA/CEQA environmental compliance
- Active registration with California contractors licensing board
- Professional liability insurance: $25M minimum
- Ability to provide full-time on-site presence during construction (4+ years)

Specific technical requirements:
- Seismic design expertise per Caltrans Seismic Design Criteria
- Bridge inspection certification (Caltrans or FHWA)
- Traffic engineering and work zone safety planning
- Caltrans Local Assistance Procedures Manual (LAPM) compliance
- Experience with Design-Bid-Build delivery method

6. TEAM REQUIREMENTS

Project team must include:

- Project Manager: PE with 20+ years highway design experience
- Lead Highway Design Engineer: PE with Caltrans project history
- Structural Engineer: SE license, bridge design specialist
- Geotechnical Engineer: PE with California seismic experience
- Environmental Compliance Manager: NEPA/CEQA expertise
- Construction Manager: 15+ years highway construction oversight
- Traffic Engineer: PE with work zone traffic control planning experience
- Minimum 8 full-time staff dedicated to construction phase

7. BUDGET

Professional Services Fee: $38 million - $45 million
(Approximately 8-9% of total construction cost)

Payment Structure:
- Design Phase: Monthly progress payments
- Construction Phase: Cost-plus-fixed-fee or lump sum (proposer's choice)
- Reimbursable expenses: Travel, testing, subconsultants

8. EVALUATION CRITERIA

Technical Qualifications (40%)
- Highway design experience and Caltrans project history
- Structural engineering capabilities
- Environmental compliance track record
- Construction management experience on active highways

Project Understanding and Approach (30%)
- Design methodology and innovation
- Construction phasing and traffic management strategy
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Schedule feasibility

Team Qualifications (20%)
- Key personnel experience and availability
- Organizational structure and staffing plan
- DBE (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise) participation plan

Cost (10%)
- Fee competitiveness and cost control approach

9. SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS

Geographic and Logistical:
- Project office must be within 50 miles of project site (Los Angeles County)
- Staff must be available for in-person meetings in Sacramento and Los Angeles
- Construction oversight requires 24/7 availability during critical phases

Regulatory and Compliance:
- All work must comply with Caltrans Highway Design Manual
- Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) oversight and approval required
- Davis-Bacon prevailing wage requirements apply
- Buy America provisions for materials
- Extensive public involvement and community engagement required

Certifications and Experience:
- Familiarity with California Public Contract Code
- Experience with Caltrans District 7 (Los Angeles) procedures
- Prior work on active urban freeways with traffic volumes >200,000 vehicles/day
- Demonstrated ability to maintain highway operations during construction
